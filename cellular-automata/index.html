minecraft & cellular automata

by annie dong

shortly after our lecture on cellular automata, i fell down a rabbit hole and found out that minecraft generates its entire world using cellular automata. apparently the actual biomes you spawn into are produced by a few simple rules running for a handful of passes over a coarse grid. this reddit thread breaks it down!all of it is stochastic cellular automata.

the sound

C418 is iconic.

i fell in love with the sounds and it inspired me to create something similar. the synth voices are bells and soft pads. the default scale is minor pentatonic, because basically every C418 track is built on pentatonic intervals. when you press play and a sparse Conway pattern triggers pentatonic bells, it does kind of sound like an oddly geometric beta minecraft soundtrack. which was my vision.

what i actually learned about CA

rules:

rules were much easier to implement than the mapping — how does a grid of dead/alive cells become music? my first attempt collapsed each generation to one beat but the fix was making the playhead sweep across the grid one column per beat, so each generation gets HEARD as 14 beats of music before it mutates. that helped turn the noise into music.

  • Conway — settles sparse and oscillates.
  • Seeds — every cell dies every step.
  • Coral — patterns only grow, never shrink.

click reseed and pick coral with a bell synth. it's nice. ♡
